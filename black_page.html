About USAF Tool
About USAF Tool

The Urban Sustainability Assessment Framework (USAF) is designed as part of the Sustainable Cities Integrated Approach Pilot (SCIAP) project which is funded by the Sixth Call of Global Environment Facility (GEF-6) and is being implemented by the United Nations Industrial Development Organisation (UNIDO) and the United Nations Human Settlements Programme (UN-Habitat), in partnership with the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) of the Government of India in five pilot project cities of India namely Bhopal, Guntur, Jaipur, Mysuru and Vijayawada. The main goal of the project is to infuse sustainability strategies into urban planning and management at the city level and create an enabling climate for investments in green infrastructure. This would reduce greenhouse gas emissions, improve service delivery, and enhance the quality of living for all citizens, thereby building resilience and strengthening the governance capacity of the cities.

The application of the USAF could enable ULBs to form a city diagnostic and devise evidence-based strategies to direct investments for low-carbon and low emission spatial planning. It could guide city managers and decision makers to adopt a data driven system for prioritizing investments in essential services and infrastructure projects by gauging their level of impact on citizens. The spatial aspect of the USAF could also help to identify under serviced areas that need attention and enable cities to build a strong pipeline of potential investment- ready projects for posing to national and/or international financial institutions.

Stakeholder Engagement Approach

The fundamental building block for sustainability and long-term impact of development interventions is to ensure participation and buy-in from communities and local governments. The USAF process is anchored in evidence-based urban planning and strengthening vertical integration of data from the local level to the central levels. Therefore, gathering and validating city level data is critical to the success of the program. It requires the support and active participation of the city leadership, including the Municipal Corporation, Development Authority, and other departments and agencies involved in decision making and data collection.

UN-Habitat’s guiding principle of the ‘People’s Process’ will be applied to the context of the SCIAP project to ensure that adequate social mobilization activities are conducted to enable various groups of stakeholders to participate and have a say in decisions that will affect their city and their lives. Agenda 2030’s core principle of ‘leave no one behind’, that emphasizes inclusion of marginalized groups of people, will also be paramount in all project activities.
